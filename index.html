OG Wolf Squad – Commander Roles V1.0 Reference

One commander at a time, wide and readable. Each strip shows your table role, deck archetypes, key synergies, and a few well-known commanders with a similar vibe.

Atlas, Distracted Hazard Destroyer
Chaotic red engine – hazard, tempo & big splashy turns
Table Instigator Aggro / Midrange Chaos & Dice Rolling Spell-Slinger Support Commander Damage Threat
Mono-Red

Table Role

  • Early: Come down swinging, pressure life totals and test removal. Atlas looks scary fast, so you decide when to lean into that or sandbag.
  • Midgame: Become the hazard pilot – rolling, flipping or otherwise gambling for big tempo swings that hit everyone.
  • Late: Convert random value into lethal – extra combats, huge single turns, or a burst of non-combat damage.
  • Social role: Natural archenemy magnet. You’re the “fun chaos player” that forces the table to cooperate or die early.

Deck Archetypes & Synergies

  • Chaos / Dice Rolling: Barbarian Class, Farideh-style payoffs, any card that rewards rolling high or rolling often.
  • Big burst turns: Extra combat steps, Seize-the-Day-type effects, and mana generation to chain multiple spells in a single “ADHD mode” turn.
  • Spell-slinger shell: Instants and sorceries that clear blockers, point burn, or dig for more gas so Atlas can keep attacking.
  • Protect the hazard: Lightning Greaves, Swiftfoot Boots, indestructible tricks. If Atlas dies twice, your whole engine slows way down.
  • Topdeck / randomness manipulation: Scry, Scroll Rack, or similar tools that quietly rig “random” outcomes in your favour.
  • Table politics: Take risky lines that help the table (shared damage, mass flips) to buy goodwill before going for lethal.

Similar Commanders

  • Etali, Primal Storm : Attacks become random free spells off everyone’s libraries.
  • Okaun, Eye of Chaos & Zndrsplt, Eye of Wisdom : Dice-rolling duo where high rolls create absurd combat swings.
  • Delina, Wild Mage : Attacks plus d20 rolls for explosive board states.
  • Neheb, the Eternal : Converts combat damage into mana for massive “one more spell” turns.
  • Norin the Wary (vibes): Chaos mascot – the board is never stable for long.
Kalbino, Master of Missteps
Face-down schemer – scry, manifest & “is it safe?” politics
Pillowfort Trickster Face-Down Matters Value Midrange Topdeck Manipulation Political Rattlesnake
Esper (WUB)

Table Role

  • Early: Develop mana rocks and cheap scry effects; start building a small army of mysterious 2/2s.
  • Midgame: Use Duckyos’s Questionable Offer plus hidden information to control combat – attacking you is always a little scary.
  • Late: Turn your manifested cards into real threats, flip high-CMC bombs at key moments, and grind value with repeated scry triggers.
  • Social role: You’re the trap player. The table spends a lot of brainpower guessing which face-down creature is actually lethal.

Deck Archetypes & Synergies

  • Face-down package: Manifest and morph creators; cards that care about face-down creatures or discount playing them.
  • Scry engines: Repeatable scry on artifacts, creatures, or lands to guarantee a manifest trigger every turn cycle.
  • ETB monsters on layaway: High-mana bombs that are safe to manifest, then flip at instant speed when it’s convenient.
  • Pillowfort tools: Effects that tax or redirect attacks so opponents are more tempted to gamble with Duckyos instead.
  • Sacrifice & recursion: Sacrifice manifested creatures for value, then reanimate the big spell you just put in the graveyard.
  • Information abuse: You know what your face-down cards are – bluff blocks, threaten combat tricks, and mislead the table.

Similar Commanders

  • Kadena, Slinking Sorcerer : Classic morph/manifest value engine.
  • Aminatou, the Fateshifter : Topdeck manipulation and blink shenanigans.
  • Roon of the Hidden Realm : Surprise blocks and flips via blink at instant speed.
  • Yennett, Cryptic Sovereign : Topdeck control where card selection is weaponised.
  • Gonti, Lord of Luxury (vibes): Hidden information and stealing value from opponents’ decks.
Jiggedy, the Recommender
Political engine – passing “Recommendations” around the table
Deal Maker Group Hug / Group Slug Artifact Subtheme Goad & Forced Combat Life & Card-Flow Manager
Jeskai Politics & Goad

Table Role

  • Early: Hand out the first Recommendation and frame it as “free value” – start building trust with one player.
  • Midgame: Rotate Recommendations to keep the table unstable: goad the scariest creature, draw cards, and push combat away from you.
  • Late: Use a critical mass of artifacts, card draw, and goaded boards to finish players already weakened.
  • Social role: You’re the content curator – suggesting who should attack where and who deserves the next Recommendation.

Deck Archetypes & Synergies

  • Political control: Curses, vote cards, and effects that reward opponents for attacking each other.
  • Artifact recursion: Recur or copy Recommendation with artifact recursion, blink, or token doublers.
  • Goad package: Extra goad so creatures are almost never allowed to sit back on defense.
  • Life-total manipulation: Small life-loss / life-gain effects that make Recommendation decisions weighty.
  • “Gifts” package: Other permanents to donate and create soft alliances.
  • Instant-speed interaction: Reward “good recommendations” and punish betrayals immediately.

Similar Commanders

  • Xantcha, Sleeper Agent : Donate a threat; everyone can pump damage and draw cards.
  • Zedruu the Greathearted : Donation deck where giving things away is value.
  • Karazikar, the Eye Tyrant : Goad-centric political commander.
  • Breena, the Demagogue : Incentivises “punch up” attacks for shared value.
  • Kwain, Itinerant Meddler (tone): Handing out resources in exchange for being left alone… for now.
Jamie, Who Points at the Crown
Monarch manager – keeps the crown moving and rewards aggression
Combat Director Monarch Matters Aggro / Token Swarm Political Midrange Tempo & Card Advantage
Mardu Monarchy

Table Role

  • Early: Establish monarchy quickly and build small evasive boards that can reliably steal the crown back.
  • Midgame: Use Jamie’s triggers to buff attackers and push the crown across the table – especially toward the “archenemy” player.
  • Late: Turn snowballed monarch draw and combat buffs into lethal swings; punish anyone who thinks they can safely sit on the crown.
  • Social role: You’re the referee, pointing at who should fight whom this turn.

Deck Archetypes & Synergies

  • Monarch package: Courts, monarch enablers, and cards that reward you for holding or stealing the crown.
  • Go-wide aggro: Token makers and cheap creatures that can repeatedly swing into the monarch.
  • Combat manipulation: Goad, forced attacks, or “can’t block” effects to keep the crown moving.
  • Threat assessment tools: Removal and interaction to back up Jamie’s political pointing.
  • Pillowfort lite: Small taxes or deterrents that make attacking you expensive unless it’s for the crown.
  • Card draw smoothing: Extra draw and rummage so you keep gas while everyone brawls.

Similar Commanders

  • Queen Marchesa : Classic monarch gameplan with politics and inevitability.
  • Adriana, Captain of the Guard : Rewards attacking multiple players with pumped boards.
  • Gahiji, Honored One (vibes): Buffs attackers to encourage aggression across the table.
  • Brimaz, Blight of Oreskos / Marneus Calgar : Token-centric combat midrange.
  • Brago, King Eternal : Who you attack each turn is a political decision.
Mamsen, Keeper of Manners
Bant etiquette – rewarding good behaviour, taxing bad behaviour
Table Mom Pillowfort / Control Food & Clue Tokens Flash / Instant-Speed Play Value Engine
Bant Politics

Table Role

  • Early: Set expectations: small incentive/punishment triggers that show what “good manners” look like.
  • Midgame: Amass Food/Clues and other tokens while tapping or redirecting the most aggressive boards.
  • Late: Turn resource tokens and counters into a dominant board – big vigilant bodies, life buffer, and card advantage.
  • Social role: You’re the teacher. You reward fair play and punish reckless shenanigans.

Deck Archetypes & Synergies

  • Food / Clue / token engines: Effects that repeatedly create Food, Treasure, or Clues to feed Mamsen’s triggers.
  • Instant-speed interaction: Counterspells, flash creatures, and tricks on opponents’ turns to maximise “good manners” rewards.
  • Pillowfort & tapdown: Propaganda-style taxes, tap effects, and “can’t attack you” clauses.
  • Value from tokens: Sac outlets and payoffs that turn your pile of tokens into cards, life, or +1/+1 counters.
  • Big vigilant bodies: Creatures that attack while still holding up defense – very “responsible gameplay”.
  • Soft group hug: Occasional shared resources (extra Food or cards) to keep the table on your side.

Similar Commanders

  • Kynaios and Tiro of Meletis : Group-hug value that slows the game and rewards civilised play.
  • Kwain, Itinerant Meddler : Tiny politics commander handing out cards and life for safety.
  • Alela, Artful Provocateur : Turns enchantments and artifacts into fliers – value from “doing the right thing”.
  • Chulane, Teller of Tales (toned down): Bant good-stuff engine wanting long, grindy games.
  • Elminster / Talion, the Kindly Lord -style commanders: Reward careful spellcasting patterns over brute force.
